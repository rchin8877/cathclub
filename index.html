CATH LAB GUIDE

Welcome to the Cath Club! Come get in the flow of the cathlab with me as I share my notes and experiences.

Keep reading to get a feel of the workflow and learn some important tips for day 1!

What to expect on your first day

  • ♡ Getting there - take the elevator to L4 and ask someone where the lockers/labs are
  • ♡ Tour of the department
  • ♡ Intro to equipment and the labs
  • ♡ Familiarising yourself with the workflow (observe how procedures begin, key tasks and teamwork with other staff)
  • ♡ LHC Examinations (bread and butter of cathlab)
  • ♡ Meeting the cardio techs, nurses and cardio regs, fellows and consultants - dw they're all very friendly! :)
  • ♡ Consolidating pre-training readings (anatomy of coronary arteries, procedure steps, equipment)
  • ♡ Don't forget to bring a small book to take notes! (Bring a pen at your own risk...)
  • ♡ Get pumped for all the new knowledge and miracles of cathlab, you got this!

Workflow

All examinations begin with first helping bring the pt into the room (check ID) and only then coming back to the console area to set up the computers. After the examination, help bring the pt out before closing the pt on the Siemens system/completing documentation. Our role in the cathlab as radiographers can at times be quite passive. However a big part of our job is to help the team and improve workflow with small actions! :)

1. Prepare Patient in the Room

  • Ideally be in room for time-out prior to pt transfer)
  • Help transfer to table
  • Set up armboard with bluey (if radial access), foot pedal, and control panel (unscrew lock on RHS and pull up and drag to the left, ensuring arrow does NOT reach end of metal bar. Leave the attached lead shield sticking out so nurse can easily drape)
  • Introduce yourself and ask for height/weight (+ pregnancy if required)
  • Confirm correct pt (ideally be there during timeout! Or check wristband)
  • Move the bed back out into holding bay, and if needed, get it ready for end of examination (e.g., flatten and prepare pat-slide)

2. Set Up Documentation

  • Open EMR cathlab list and add documentation for radiation dose and protocol (do this first to ensure EMR accurately displays the dose as per time!)
    • Open patient file by double-clicking the first column arrow
    • Go to documentation and click the + sign to add a new entry
    • Title: Radiation dose and protocol (cardiac)
    • Using auto-text (supervisor will show you how to set up later), pre-fill the area being x-rayed (usually thorax), date, and consultant's name
    • Save (only sign at the end once dose details are typed in. Once saved, the title cannot be edited so double-check!)
  • Open Microsoft Teams and add the case to the excel spreadsheet (for our own record-keeping purposes)
    • Type in key details including start time of the case

3. Set Up X-ray Equipment

  • Open patient on Siemens
    • Find the patient on scheduler (will only appear if arrived by cardio tech!)
      • Reference with sticker and enter carefully:

        • (Will be used for dose modulation! Can enter in feet/inches and convert to metric on the system!)

        • ❗ They will be annoyed if incorrect ❗

  • Bring the tube in by selecting bottom left button (custom preset 1) and pushing joystick forward.
  • Once the cardiologist has inserted the sheath (will pull out a green tube and put on teggy), you can turn down the lights.
  • Open the patient on the Sony screen (controls what appears on monitors inside and outside the lab)
    • Set haemo on the screen inside (lab) and left CR (control room) screens
    • Bring up previous imaging on connected PC and be ready to display on Sony 1 if needed
    • Change the screen display setting (e.g., half/half live and assist screens)

4. During the Examination

Changes depending on procedures and cardiologists workflow, but generally:

  • Be ready to save reference images (e.g. contrast runs showing stenosis) in initial runs. Otherwise, can open on syngo and take note of good reference images, and go back quickly to reference when Dr busy setting up for intervention.
  • Listen out for the cardiologist asking to 'fluorosave' (saving fluoro runs, not needed for acquisitions which are auto-saved. Typically when a wire has been inserted, balloon inflated)
  • Collimate to the border of the heart when possible and move filter to block glare
  • Start on 25 mag, then go down to 20 once engaged in artery
  • Be ready to change the screen display setting (e.g. if using IVUS select a format with Sony)
  • Change acquisition rate/dose (e.g. BMI+ for spider view)
  • Label images and send to PACS (or take note of run # and label at the end)

5. Ending Examination

  • Disable radiation and bring the tube out (preset position: transfer patient)
  • Remove plastic cover over II and store it in the drawers to re-use as foot pedal cover!
  • Help transfer patient back into bed
    • Bring the bed back in
    • Remove armboard (place on end of bed) and move pedal out of the way
    • Wipe down armboards, table and tube*. Help the tech wipe down the BP cuff and ECG leads if they are busy too.
    • Change bin liners (bottom corner cupboards, fit with the blue band)
    • Grab new bedsheet, blanket, and 2 saline bags for the techs from the adjacent storeroom if they haven't already got it themselves
  • Close patient on the computer after they have left the room ❗ once closed cannot be opened again ❗ before going back to fill in the excel spreadsheet
    • Add any intervention performed in comments (e.g., Stent x 2 to LAD)
    • Update the procedure if necessary (e.g., LHC PCI)
    • Complete EMR documentation (click sign to complete)

    *For end of day clean, ROTATE THE TUBE so you can clean the very bottom which is usually dirty!
